Panipuri

Mouth watering Panipuri(The flavour blaster).

Panipuri, also known as golgappa or puchka, is a popular street snack in India.It consists of a small, hollow, crispy fried or baked shell, filled with a mixture of spiced mashed potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and tamarind chutney. The shell is then punctured on top and filled with a mixture of spicy and tangy water made with mint, coriander, cumin, and other spices. The entire snack is meant to be eaten in one bite, creating a burst of flavors and textures in the mouth.

Ingredients üìù

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 2 medium-sized boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup tamarind chutney
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 2 cups chilled water
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves

Instructions ü´ï

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Add enough water to make a stiff dough. Knead the dough for a few minutes until it becomes smooth.
  2. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  3. After 20 minutes, knead the dough again for a few minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into small circles. Cut the circles using a cookie cutter or a lid to make uniform shapes.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat. When the oil is hot, add the circles and fry them until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  6. Remove the fried circles from the oil and drain them on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  7. Mix the mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, chopped coriander leaves, roasted cumin powder, red chili powder, chaat masala, and salt in a bowl.
  8. Make a small hole on top of each fried circle and fill it with the potato and chickpea mixture.
  9. Add a little tamarind chutney to each filled circle.
  10. In a separate bowl, mix chilled water, mint leaves, and a little salt to make the spicy and tangy water.
  11. Serve the panipuri with the spicy and tangy water. To eat, dip the filled panipuri in the water and enjoy it in one bite.

Your homemade panipuri is ready to enjoy!
